# 0000. Decision Title

<!--
Writing criteria

- Record one architecturally significant decision. Name the decision, not merely
  the problem, in the title.
- Write for a future maintainer who was not present. Lead with why the decision
  became necessary; the motivation will usually outlive the implementation.
- Aim for 200–600 words and no more than one or two pages. Use extra space only
  when genuine alternatives or consequences need it.
- Use plain, complete sentences and short paragraphs. Keep Context factual and
  value-neutral; state the Decision directly in definitive language.
- Record the stable choice, not a complete architecture description or an
  implementation handbook. Link to conventions and design docs for details that
  may evolve.
- Include positive, negative, and neutral consequences. Do not sell the choice.
- Include rejected alternatives only when they were seriously considered, and
  explain fairly why each lost in this context.
- Once accepted, do not rewrite the decision. Supersede it with a new ADR and
  preserve the history.

## Context

Why did this decision become necessary? Describe the facts, constraints, and
competing forces a future maintainer will need in order to understand it.

## Decision

State the stable choice directly. Keep implementation rules in the relevant
convention or design document.

## Consequences

What becomes easier, harder, or explicitly out of scope? Include costs and
limitations, not only benefits.

## Rejected Alternatives

Include only genuine contenders. Explain briefly and fairly why each was not
chosen for this project.

## References

- Add links to related docs, issues, pull requests, or external references.
